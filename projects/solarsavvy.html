SolarSavvy

A data-driven platform to improve solar energy accessibility for low-income Indian homeowners — bridging the gap between awareness and action.

Aug 2025 – Present
Solo Developer

🟢 Active

01. the problem

India's PM Surya Ghar scheme offers significant subsidies for rooftop solar installation — yet adoption among low-income households remains critically low. The barriers aren't financial alone.

Key insight: People who qualified for subsidies were still not acting. The gap wasn't awareness — it was trust, complexity, and lack of personalised information. This is a behavioural problem, not just a technical one.

Existing tools were either too generic or required technical expertise to use. No solution addressed the specific needs of low-income homeowners in Tier 2 and Tier 3 Indian cities.

02. the solution

SolarSavvy lets users input their address and get an instant, personalised estimate of their rooftop solar potential, installation cost, and 10-year ROI — in plain language.

05. lessons learned

  • The hardest problems are behavioural, not technical — understanding why people don't act matters more than building features.
  • ML accuracy of ±12% with minimal data is achievable when features are carefully engineered from indirect signals.
  • Simplicity beats sophistication for social impact tools — users need trust, not complexity.
  • Combining engineering with policy knowledge (PM Surya Ghar subsidies) creates real-world relevance.
